Kimberly Wong

Kimberly Wong

Operations and Logistics | Class of 2018

Work Term Employer(s): BC Tech Association (2 Terms)


How many jobs did you apply for before you landed your first Co-op placement?

I applied for around 25 jobs before landing, but it felt like 1000!


How has the Co-op team helped you?

From Conny tolerating me bothering her way more times than could ever be necessary (and always being patient), to Lynne connecting me with other Co-op students working with companies I was interested in, to Nicole and Mursal finding the BC Tech job posting and literally standing in front of me convincing me that it was a perfect fit, everyone on the Co-op team has been an amazing help. In case you were wondering, Nicole and Mursal were right – BC Tech is full of extremely passionate individuals who exactly when to put on their serious thinking hats, and when it’s time for an Earnest Ice Cream break.


What are your biggest tips for newly admitted Co-op students?

  • Keep an open mind when applying for jobs – you’re likely in Co-op because you want to gain experience, try to new things, and maybe just find your passion (or two). Remember that to do those things, you may need to step out of the Sauder-made divisions of specializations. If I had continued only pursuing OpLog-specific jobs, I may never have gained my newfound love for tech and the #startuplife.
  • Whether you’re nervous about job-hunting, you have an interview coming up that you want help preparing for, or anything in between, make an appointment with the Co-op office. Mock interviews aren’t as awkward as they may seem, and can be extremely helpful. The Co-op team is there to help you succeed – don’t ever feel like you can’t go to them!

    Suzanne Liu

    Marketing | Class of 2018

    Work Term Employer(s): PepsiCo Foods Canada (2 Terms)


    How many jobs did you apply for before you landed your first Co-op placement?

    7


    How has the Co-op team helped you?

    I visited the Co-op team often during the application phase to get resume/ CL touch ups as well as interviewing tips. I even visited career coaches for the smallest things such as how to phrase a follow up email. They made my job hunting process a lot smoother!


    What is your biggest accomplishment in Co-op?

    Acquiring 26 new accounts for the PepsiCo Foods Team. I traveled the lower mainland pitching the Snacks To You program to small business owners. At the end of my project, I was glad I met so many interesting customers who are just as excited about Pepsi products as I am!


    What is the best part of the Co-op program?

    It provides you with the chance to try out different career paths and test the waters. By structuring work terms to be either 4 or 8 months, you really can experience different companies and industries without any commitment. The program allows us to find the right career that really caters to our interests and strengths.


    Future plans after graduation??

    TBD! Hopefully Co-op will help me figure it out.


    What are your biggest tips for newly admitted Co-op students?

    Don’t be scared to apply for positions you feel that you’re not qualified for. You never know what opportunities are available to you. And don’t get discouraged if your first few applications don’t go the way you planned. It’s all part of the learning process and it’ll only get easier.

    Bert Li

    Finance and BTM | Class of 2018

    Work Term Employer(s): Hootsuite Media Inc. , WorkSafe BC


    How many jobs did you apply for before you landed your first Co-op placement?

    Around 30 applications and around 8 interviews – I may be the worst interviewee in all of Co-op.


    How has the Co-op team helped you?

  • The Co-op team has helped greatly with the job hunt: resume, cover letter, interview, network.
  • The Co-op team provided critical emotional support through the endless application grind.

    • What is your biggest accomplishment in Co-op?

    Playing a key role in redesigning and implementing a method of capturing customer data and feedback for Hootsuite. This contributed to automating the process as well as increasing a company KPI (Early NPS Score) by gathering more valuable information.


    What is the best part of the Co-op program?

  • It helps with figuring out what type of work, culture, and balance you desire most.
  • It’s sometimes easier to enter competitive companies as a Co-op student.
  • Delays your graduation and the inevitable quarter life crisis that follows after graduating and facing eternal full time.

    • Future plans after graduation??

    Find a job that makes me happy while making me a solid living, be challenged while being comfortable at work, fall in love, get married, have two kids, buy a house, retire doing the things I love. As you can see I’m pretty lost – hopefully Co-op will help.


    What are your biggest tips for newly admitted Co-op students?

    APPLY EARLY because interviews and offers are handed out often before the deadline.

    John Yoo

    Business and Computer Science | Class of 2018

    Work Term Employer(s): General Dynamics, SAP


    How many jobs did you apply for before you landed your first Co-op placement?

    Approximately 30.


    How has the Co-op team helped you?

    After helping you figure out your professional goals, values, and skills, the team points you in the right direction with information about ideal careers, be it in the form of info sessions, mentorship programs, or other related events. The co-op team also provides opportunities to strengthen your resume/cover letter and prepare for interviews, ultimately making you a more confident applicant.


    What is your biggest accomplishment in Co-op?

    I programmed 8 features in two software version releases for the Aurora CP-140, a military plane currently deployed by the Canadian Armed Forces. I would speak more about them but it’s classified.


    What is the best part of the Co-op program?

    For me, I’m still not entirely sure what it is exactly that I want to do. The tech industry has an overabundance of specialized positions and responsibilities that it’s difficult to pinpoint a specific role that I would see myself doing for the next five years. Co-op has given me the opportunity to explore these specialized roles both explicitly through the job postings, and through the position at GD I’ve held as a software engineer, where coding was only one of many responsibilities I’ve had.


    Future plans after graduation??

    Once I’m sure I’ve gotten my foot in the door, I plan to take a really, really long vacation.


    What are your biggest tips for newly admitted Co-op students?

    Apply early. You don’t want your interviews to conflict with your midterm schedule.
